Affirmative Action

**11.1 Office of the Provost - Affirmative Action Handbook**

The Office of the Provost is prepared to offer a variety of assistance
to all academic units in their efforts to attract scholars who are
women, minorities and persons with disabilities to Michigan State
University. Within the  [Office of the Provost, the Associate Provost
and Associate Vice President for Academic Human
Resources](https://provost.msu.edu/provosts/hr.html "Provost's Office")
 (353-5300) is charged with the primary responsibility of aiding units
and colleges with the recruitment and professional enrichment of all
faculty, with particular emphasis on women and minorities. This
individual is available to meet candidates who are being seriously
considered for faculty appointments, to discuss the University\'s
expectations for promotion and tenure, and to describe the various
affirmative action support services for faculty. If assistance is
desired in recruiting or in creating faculty development programs, the
Associate Provost and Associate Vice President for Academic Human
Resources should be contacted.\
