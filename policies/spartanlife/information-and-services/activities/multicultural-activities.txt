MULTICULTURAL ACTIVITIES


The diversity of American society can be explored through traditional
campus events. Many annual and special activities celebrate the
many heritages of Native Americans, Latinos, African-Americans, and
Asian Pacific Americans. Women’s History Month is observed in March and
National Coming Out Days, held in October, celebrates Lesbian, Bisexual,
Gay and Transgender identities. Events are well publicized. For
information contact: Office of Cultural and Academic Transitions, 339
Student Services Building, (517) 353-7745; LBGT Resource Center, 302
Student Services Building, (517) 353-9520; or Mosiac; the Multicultural
Unity Center, MSU Union (517) 432-7153. See also _Services for Community
Groups_.

MOSAIC: THE MULTICULTURAL UNITY CENTER: Mosaic is located on the 2nd
floor of the MSU Union. The Center is a space where students can come to
study, hold meetings, attend different intercultural programs and
events, and a place to get information about opportunities to connect
with others from different cultural communities. Hours are Monday
through Thursday, 9:00 a.m. to 11:00 p.m.; Friday, 9:00 a.m. to 7:00
p.m.; and Sunday, Noon to 11:00 p.m. For information, call (517)
432-7153_._
