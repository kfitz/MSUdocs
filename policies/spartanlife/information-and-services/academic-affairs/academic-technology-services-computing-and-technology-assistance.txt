TECHNOLOGY RESOURCES


MSU IT SERVICE DESK consultants provide technology support by telephone,
chat, email, and online forms for general computer questions,
troubleshooting computer issues and configuring personal computers to
work with the MSU network and mail system. For assistance call (517)
432-6200/(844) 678-6200, email ithelp@msu.edu or visit
_https://tech.msu.edu/support/help_.

24-HOUR DISTANCE LEARNING SERVICES provides support for the D2L course
management system and other learning tools including start-up, technical
and access problems, and feature requests. Help with accessing online
resources using EZ Proxy, book and article delivery, and help with
online courses is also available. Call (517) 355-2345 or (800) 500-1554
or visit _www.lib.msu.edu/dls_.

COMPUTER LABS are located throughout campus giving students access to
the Internet, popular and specialized software, and printing services.
Anyone with an MSU NetID may use these computers at no charge. To locate
a computer lab on campus, visit
_https://tech.msu.edu/computerlabs/locations_. Most public computer labs
are wheelchair accessible. For detailed information regarding
accommodations for persons with disabilities, contact the Resource
Center for Persons with Disabilities at (517) 884-7273.

MSU TECH STORE, in room 110 of the Computer Center offers educational
discounts on computers, software, and accessories. The Tech Store also
provides walk-in help for face-to-face consulting and assistance with
repairs. Find out more at _https://techstore.msu.edu_ or call (517)
432-0700.

MSU EMAIL addresses are issued to students when they are admitted to
MSU. This is the only email address that the university will use for
sending official communications to students. If you’re an undergraduate
student, log in using your MSU NetID and password at
_spartanmail.msu.edu_. If you’re a graduate student, log in using your
MSU NetID and password at _mail.msu.edu._ For more information, read the
Student Email Communications Notice at
_https://tech.msu.edu/about/guidelines-policies/student-email-communications/_.

MSU WIRELESS provides wireless Internet access in the main Library, the
Union, general common areas of residence halls, and a growing number of
public spaces, classrooms, and meeting spaces around campus. For
additional information, including which residence halls have access to
Wi-Fi in student rooms, visit _https://tech.msu.edu/wireless_.

MSU SECUREIT (_https://secureit.msu.edu_) provides resources for
students to learn more about creating strong passwords, avoiding
phishing scams, and security best practices. Students can also report
suspicious issues or fraudulent emails by contacting the MSU IT Service
Desk at (517) 432-6200 or emailing ithelp@msu.edu.

FILE SHARING: Do not share copyrighted music, movies, or any other files
online. It is a serious violation of MSU’s Acceptable Use Policy
(_https://tech.msu.edu/about/guidelines-policies/aup_) and federal law
to share copyrighted files from your computer. Illegal file sharing and
violations can lead to suspension, expulsion, and/or lawsuits. For more
information, visit _https://secureit.msu.edu/file-copyright/index.html_.
For details on how MSU addresses suspected file sharing when the
university receives Digital Millennium Copyright Act (DMCA) notices,
visit
_https://tech.msu.edu/guidelines-policies/procedures-copyright-infringement/_.

Visit _tech.msu.edu/students_ for the most current information on MSU
technology resources and support available to students. Visit
_http://tech.msu.edu/new-students_ for getting started technology
information.
