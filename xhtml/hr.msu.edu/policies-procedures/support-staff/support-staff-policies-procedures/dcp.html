457(b) Deferred Compensation Plan Policy & Procedure

(Revised 09/16)

Policy Statement

The University provides a voluntary 457(b) Deferred Compensation Plan for eligible employees.

Eligibility: Regular full-time (90-100%) and part-time (50-89.9%) faculty, academic staff, executive management, and University support staff in a benefits eligible position.  Benefits eligible positions for faculty, academic staff and executive management are defined as 50% or greater employment for a continuous period of 9 months or longer.  Benefits eligible positions for support staff are defined as regular full-time (90-100%) and part-time (50-89.9%) employment without an end date of less than 9 months.

Effective date of benefit: Eligible employees may elect participation upon employment or any time during employment.

Summary of benefits and features:

  • The benefit provides income during retirement and benefit payments to the beneficiary in the event of death prior to retirement.
  • Contributions are on a tax-deferred basis contributed to an account held at a retirement vendor, and are 100% vested.
  • Contributions cease with the last day of paid employment, a change to an ineligible employment status, or designation by the employee to stop future contributions.
  • Withdrawals are allowed after retirement, or termination, regardless of age or length of service.
  • In-service withdrawals are subject to IRS guidelines and are permitted due to "unforeseen emergency"
  • Loan options may be available. Contact the retirement vendor for specific information on loans.
  • A more detailed summary of benefits and features is available in the online Enrollment Guide.
  • The MSU 457(b) Deferred Compensation Plan Document provides more detail and governs the administration of the Plan.

Procedure

Application for enrollment:

  • Employees may enroll at any time after becoming eligible. Enroll online at the EBS Portal.  Review the Enrollment Instructions for more detail.
  • The enrollment process will be effective either the current pay period or the next pay period, depending on the payroll processing date.  
  • Retroactive enrollment is not permitted.

Changes in coverage or personal information:

  • Employees may make changes by accessing the EBS Portal. Review the Enrollment and Changes Instructions for more detail.
  • Employees may make changes to their retirement account by contacting their Investment Vendor.

Refer questions to: MSU Human Resources (telephone 517-353-4434 or e-mail solutionscenter@hr.msu.edu)
